Orbit types

Circular orbits have no eccentricity and radial velocity, the velocity vector is fully tangential.

Elliptic orbits have eccentricities between 0 and 1, thus having a radial velocity component except at periapsis and apoapsis.

Parabolas are unbound orbits, with an eccentricity of 1. Their excess velocity with respect to the central body is 0.

Hyperbolas have eccentricities greater than 1 and are also unbound orbits. Their excess velocity will be non-zero at infinity relative to the central body.
